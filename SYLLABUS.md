# SYLLABUS.md — MS Amma's Carnatic Vocal Curriculum

**A master-designed, graded curriculum for teaching Carnatic vocal music to a child.**
Authored as the pedagogical backbone for the AI teacher "MS Amma." *Ganamrudha Bodhini*
(Panchapakesa Iyer) is **one resource within this curriculum, not its boundary.** MS Amma teaches
from this syllabus — drawing on the book where it fits, and going beyond it freely with the
judgment of a PhD-level guru.

> **Current student:** Aruvi, age 7 — Sarali ✓, Janta ✓, in **Stage 3 (first compositions / Geethams)**.

---

## 1. Teaching philosophy (how a guru teaches, not how a textbook is read)

1. **Ear first, notation second.** Carnatic music is an oral tradition (*guru-shishya parampara*).
   MS Amma sings a phrase; the child sings it back. Notation supports memory; it never leads.
2. **Embodied rhythm.** Tala is *kept in the body* (clap/wave/finger-count), never abstract. Every
   piece is practiced with the hand keeping tala from day one.
3. **Sruti is sacred.** Always align to a fixed Sa (tambura/shruti drone) before singing. Train the
   child to *find* Sa, not just be told it.
4. **Joyful repetition over drilling.** Mastery comes from happy, varied repetition — change tempo,
   add a story, make it a game — never the same dull explanation a 4th time.
5. **Bhakti & bhava.** Compositions carry meaning and devotion. Teach the *story* and the *feeling*,
   not just the syllables — this is what makes a musician, not a parrot.
6. **Voice culture for a child.** Protect a 7-year-old's voice: comfortable range (~C4–G5 / madhya
   sthayi), no strain, short held notes, lots of breath play. Pitch *accuracy* matters more than
   range or volume at this age.
7. **Gamaka is the soul — introduced gradually.** Early stages sing plain (suddha) swaras; gamakas
   (the characteristic oscillations) are layered in only once the skeleton is secure. **Never treat
   an intended gamaka as a pitch error.**
8. **Praise the specific.** "அந்த Ri-வை perfect-ஆ பிடித்தாய்!" beats a generic "good." Correct the
   one thing that matters most, then move on.

## 2. Curriculum structure

Four parallel tracks advance together. The **Stage** (below) is set by the Repertoire track; the
other three tracks layer onto whatever stage the student is in.

| Track | What it builds |
|---|---|
| **A. Repertoire & Technique** | The graded path of exercises and compositions (Stages 0–8). |
| **B. Laya (Rhythm/Tala)** | Hand-kept tala, from clapping to the Sapta Talas and gati. |
| **C. Aural / Manodharma seeds** | Ear training, raga recognition, and the seeds of improvisation. |
| **D. Theory & Appreciation** | Swaras, ragas, the Melakarta system, talas, composers, history. |

---

## 3. Track A — Repertoire & Technique (the graded stages)

### Stage 0 — Sruti & Voice *(continuous foundation, never "finished")*
- Find and hold **Sa** against the drone; match a sung note.
- Open, relaxed tone; breath control; clap a steady pulse.
- **Outcome:** sings a clean, in-tune held Sa-Pa-Sa and keeps a steady beat.

### Stage 1 — Sarali Varisai *(Abhyasa Gana I)* — **Aruvi ✓**
- The 14 Sarali Varisai exercises in **Mayamalavagowla** raga.
- Sung in increasing speeds (first, second, third kalai).
- **Outcome:** all 12 swarasthanas placed accurately; steady tala throughout.

### Stage 2 — Janta, Dhatu & Alankaras *(Abhyasa Gana II)* — **Aruvi ✓ (Janta); Alankaras next**
- **Janta Varisai** (paired/doubled swaras) ✓.
- **Dhatu / Melsthayi–Keezhsthayi Varisai** (octave-jump exercises) — strengthens range & accuracy.
- **Sapta Tala Alankaras** — the seven principal talas as exercises.
- **Outcome:** confident octave movement; can sing exercises in the seven talas keeping hand-tala.

### Stage 3 — First Compositions *(Gana I — Geethams)* — **Aruvi is HERE**
- **Pillari Geethams** (Purandara Dasa) — the traditional first compositions:
  *Sri Gananatha* (Malahari, Adi), *Kundagaura* (Malahari), *Kereya Neeranu*, *Padumanabha*,
  *Sri Ramachandra*.
- **Sadharana & Lakshana Geethams** in Malahari, Mayamalavagowla, etc.
- These join sahitya (words) to swara for the first time; still plain swaras, simple tala.
- **Outcome:** sings 4–6 geethams from memory, in tune, with hand-tala and correct words.

### Stage 4 — Swarajati & Jatiswaram
- **Jatiswarams** (swara passages, no sahitya) and **Swarajatis** (swara + sahitya, more structure).
- New ragas appear: **Bilahari, Kalyani, Saveri, Khamas**, etc.
- First **sangatis** (melodic variations) and slightly faster laya.
- **Outcome:** handles multi-section pieces and 2–3 new ragas; cleaner rhythmic phrasing.

### Stage 5 — Varnams *(the discipline milestone)*
- **Tana Varnams** — e.g. *Ninnukori* (Mohanam), *Sami Ninne* (Sri Ranjani/Begada), *Era Napai*
  (Todi), *Viriboni* (Bhairavi, advanced).
- Sung in two speeds; the rigorous foundation for all higher singing. Gamakas now taught in earnest.
- **Outcome:** sings at least one varnam in two speeds with correct gamakas and tala — a real
  graduation point.

### Stage 6 — Krithis I *(beginner kirtanas — the heart of the repertoire)*
- Simple **krithis / kirtanas** from the great composers:
  - **Trinity:** Tyagaraja, Muthuswami Dikshitar, Syama Sastri (start simple, e.g. Tyagaraja's
    *Pancharatna*-adjacent easy pieces, Dikshitar's *Vatapi Ganapatim* later).
  - **Purandara Dasa** devaranamas, **Annamacharya** kirtanas, simple **bhajans**.
- Structure: Pallavi – Anupallavi – Charanam; introduce **bhava** (emotion) and meaning.
- **Outcome:** 3–5 krithis from memory with basic gamakas, sangatis, and understanding of the words.

### Stage 7 — Krithis II + Manodharma seeds
- More krithis across more ragas; richer sangatis and gamakas.
- First **kalpana swaram** (improvised swara patterns to a tala) and simple **niraval**.
- **Raga identification** by ear.
- **Outcome:** sings krithis musically; can do a short kalpana swaram set in a familiar raga.

### Stage 8 — Manodharma & Concert craft *(advanced)*
- **Alapana** (raga elaboration), **niraval**, extended **kalpana swaram**, **Ragam-Tanam-Pallavi**.
- Complex talas and **gati bhedam**; **kutcheri (concert) format** and stage presence.
- **Outcome:** can present a small concert set independently.

## 4. Track B — Laya / Tala (rhythm)

Advances alongside repertoire. **Adi Thaalam is v1's focus** (see CLAUDE.md / Requirments §7).

1. **Pulse & clap** — steady beat, clap on the beat (Stage 0–1).
2. **Adi Thaalam** — 8 beats, **Chaturasra Laghu (4) + 2 Drutams (2+2)**, kept with the hand:
   Beat 1 *Thattu* (clap), 2–4 little/ring/middle finger taps, 5 *Veechu* (wave), 6 clap, 7 wave,
   8 clap → repeat. (Stage 2–3.)
3. **Rupaka, Misra Chapu, Khanda Chapu** — common concert talas (Stage 4–5).
4. **Sapta Talas → the 35 talas**, anga structure, then **gati/nadai bhedam** (Stage 6–8).
- **Aruvi now:** Adi Thaalam, 30→60→90 BPM, hand pattern + singing simultaneously.

---

## 5. Track C — Aural training & Manodharma seeds

- **Find Sa** against the drone; sing it back (every stage).
- **Interval / swara recognition** — name a sung swara; sing a named swara.
- **Call-and-response** — MS Amma sings a phrase, Aruvi echoes (core to every lesson).
- **Swara dictation** — write/say the swaras of a short heard phrase (Stage 4+).
- **Raga recognition** — name the raga of a familiar phrase (Stage 6+).
- **Kalpana swaram & niraval** improvisation (Stage 7+).

---

## 6. Track D — Theory & Appreciation *(woven in, never front-loaded)*

Teach as stories and discoveries, not lectures. A 7-year-old learns these as games.

- **Swaras:** the 7 swaras (Sa Ri Ga Ma Pa Da Ni) and the **12 swarasthanas**.
- **Raga basics:** arohanam / avarohanam; a raga is a "musical personality."
- **Tala system:** the **Sapta Talas**, anga (laghu/drutam/anudrutam), → the 35 talas, jaati, gati.
- **Melakarta system (72):** the parent-raga framework and **janya** (derived) ragas — introduced
  much later (Stage 6+), simply.
- **Gamakas:** the ten kinds (*dasavidha gamakas*) — the ornaments that make Carnatic music alive
  (Stage 5+).
- **Composers & history:** the **Trinity** (Tyagaraja, Dikshitar, Syama Sastri), Purandara Dasa
  ("Pitamaha"/grandfather of Carnatic music), Annamacharya, Swati Tirunal; the *bhakti* tradition.
- **Sruti & laya** as the two pillars; **instruments** (tambura, veena, mridangam, violin);
  **concert etiquette**.

## 7. Daily practice (sadhakam) — what MS Amma assigns

A child's daily ~15–20 min, scaled by attention:
1. **Warm-up (2–3 min):** hold Sa to the drone; one Sarali set.
2. **Current lesson (5–8 min):** the active geetham/krithi, slowly then at tempo, with hand-tala.
3. **Revision (3–4 min):** an earlier piece, to keep it alive.
4. **Tala-only (2–3 min):** if rhythm was shaky — Adi Thaalam practice.
5. **Joy song (2 min):** a fun favourite, to end smiling.

---

## 8. Assessment philosophy *(aligns with the app's "guidance-first" scoring)*

- Judge **sruti (pitch) and laya (tala)** first — these are the non-negotiables.
- Early stages: **plain-swara accuracy**; do not penalize the absence of gamakas, and never flag an
  *intended* gamaka as an error.
- A piece is "ready to advance" when sung **from memory, in tune, in tala, with correct sahitya** —
  confirmed across two sessions and **unlocked by the parent** (never auto-advanced).
- Feedback is always **specific + encouraging**, max 2–3 points at a time (see CLAUDE.md §2).
